This privacy policy applies to the mobile game Cairo Force (the “App”), developed and published by salfedev (“we”, “us”), and distributed via Google Play. It explains what data the App handles, how, and your choices.

1. Summary

  • Cairo Force is playable fully offline. The core game does not require an account.
  • Game progress and settings are stored locally on your device only.
  • On Android, the App sends privacy-preserving gameplay analytics to Google Firebase Analytics (see section 4). We do not send your name, email, or free-text messages with these events.
  • The optional online leaderboard is opt-in and uses Google Firebase (see section 3).
  • We do not sell your data, and we do not share it with third parties except the service providers listed below.

2. Data stored on your device

The App saves your game progress (campaign clears, stars, unlocks, scores), gameplay settings, and tuning preferences in a local save file on your device. This data never leaves your device unless you use the online features described in sections 3 and 4. Uninstalling the App deletes it.

3. Optional online leaderboard (Firebase)

If you choose to use the online leaderboard, the App uses Google Firebase (Authentication and Cloud Firestore) to store and display scores. As of build 1.7.263, the leaderboard includes two boards:

  • Endless — your best Endless wave score and the time it was submitted;
  • Campaign — your total campaign star count and the time it was submitted.

For both boards, we also store:

  • an anonymous account identifier (or your Google account identity when you sign in or link Google to an existing guest account — the same identifier is kept so scores and display name carry over);
  • the display name you choose for the leaderboard.

This data is used solely to operate the leaderboard. If you never open the leaderboard or never submit a score, no leaderboard data is created.

4. Gameplay analytics (Firebase Analytics)

On Android builds distributed via Google Play (and equivalent test builds), the App uses Google Firebase Analytics to understand how the game is used and to improve stability and balance. Analytics is on by default and runs without requiring an account.

What we send from the App — gameplay events at run boundaries only (never continuously while you play):

  • session_start — when you reach the main menu after launch (includes device model, OS version, screen size, locale, and app version — no personal identity);
  • session_end — when you leave the App or send it to the background (includes session length in seconds and whether you were in a run or on the main menu);
  • stage_clear — when you complete a stage (includes map identifier, star count, and run duration in seconds);
  • game_over — when a run ends in defeat (includes map identifier and wave reached).

We do not intentionally collect or send your real name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, precise location, or free-text chat through these events.

What Google may collect. Firebase Analytics is operated by Google LLC. As with most mobile analytics SDKs, Google may also process standard device and app-instance identifiers and technical metadata needed to deliver the service (for example app version and device model). See the Firebase Privacy and Security documentation and Google’s Privacy Policy for details.

Your choices. Analytics is controlled by an in-app setting stored on your device (analytics_enabled, default on). An in-app toggle in Settings is planned; until then, you may contact us (section 11) to request that we note an opt-out for your device or to ask how to disable collection on your install. When analytics is off, new events are not sent.

Editor, desktop, and headless builds do not send analytics events.

5. Data we do not collect

  • No precise location data.
  • No contacts, photos, files, microphone, or camera access.
  • No advertising identifiers — the current version of the App contains no advertising.
  • No in-app purchases in the current version.

Future versions may add optional rewarded advertising and in-app purchases. If and when those features ship, this policy will be updated before release, the “Last updated” date will change, and any legally required consent (for example GDPR consent in the EEA/UK) will be requested in-app before ad or purchase data is processed.

6. Data retention and deletion

  • Local save data: retained on your device until you delete it (in-game “New Game” or uninstalling the App).
  • Leaderboard data: retained while the leaderboard service operates. You can request deletion of your leaderboard entry and associated account identifier at any time by emailing us (below); we will remove it within 30 days.
  • Analytics data: processed and retained by Google under Firebase Analytics retention settings. We do not maintain a separate copy of raw analytics logs. You may use Google’s account and privacy tools where applicable, or contact us if you have questions.

7. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has submitted personal data via the leaderboard, contact us and we will delete it.

8. Security

Online features transmit data over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS). Leaderboard data is stored in Google Firebase with access rules restricting writes to authenticated sessions. Analytics events are sent to Google’s Firebase infrastructure. No payment or password data is handled by the App.

9. Your rights

Depending on your region (including the EEA and UK under GDPR), you may have the right to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you, and the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

Personal data we may hold directly includes leaderboard data described in section 3. Analytics processing is primarily handled by Google as described in section 4. Contact us to exercise your rights or to request leaderboard deletion or analytics opt-out assistance.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the App evolves (for example when advertising or in-app purchases are added). Material changes will be reflected on this page with a new “Last updated” date before they take effect.
